My work weaves different areas of practice, reflecting how data visualisation bridges and converges fields. The sections below follow this thread, from visual communication and analytical tools to interaction design, mapping and sustainability.

Data visualisation and storytelling

I approach data visualisation as a way of structuring and communicating complex information, drawing on experience across data-driven stories, dashboards, reporting, editorial visuals and custom-coded work. This also involves developing standards, guidance and training that shape how visualisation is used and understood across organisations, ensuring it is applied meaningfully and with impact.

Dashboard and tool development

At the core of dashboard and tool development is the design of systems that structure and support the effective use of information over time, whether working with business intelligence platforms or fully custom-coded tools. My experience in the software industry is complemented by work across sectors, developing a wide range of data products – from senior leadership dashboards and organisational reporting to campaign and social media performance tracking, policy monitoring and economic scenario analysis.

Human–computer interaction design

Human–computer interaction underpins my work. Whether developing a dashboard, an infographic or a custom tool, my focus is on how people encounter, interpret and act on information. This means designing interfaces that are intuitive without being simplistic, and that support exploration, comparison and decision-making. UX and UI design are not separate stages in my process, but integral to ensuring analytical work is usable in practice.

Project and people management

I lead and coordinate data visualisation work across multidisciplinary teams, managing projects from definition through delivery. This includes working closely with data scientists and analysts to shape analytical outputs into usable data products, as well as managing a small design function comprising both in-house and external designers.

GIS and mapping

Geography is a key dimension of the analytical work I oversee, providing a consistent thread across disciplines and areas of analysis. I build custom maps and use geographic data to surface relevant patterns and context across regions. This practice is also shaped by my background as a place-policy analyst, where spatial evidence was needed to support funding decisions, infrastructure planning and long-term strategy under public scrutiny.

Sustainability and accountability

My experience in sustainability has shaped how I understand the risks, trade-offs and opportunities organisations face today across environmental, economic and regulatory contexts. It has also influenced my approach to analytical work more broadly, particularly where evidence is used to support strategic decisions, accountability or public-facing communication.
