Project Tempo Regional Policy Dashboards

Data tools and regional insights

This project involved designing and building interactive analytical dashboards to support policy engagement across European countries, starting with a regional deep dive for Italy. I led the dashboard design and development using Tableau—the established analytical platform across Project Tempo at this stage—translating survey-based measures of public support for different energy technologies into tools that allow stakeholders to understand how public sentiment, infrastructure constraints and policy ambition intersect at regional level.

Through close collaboration with data scientists, economists and policy teams, the dashboards were expanded beyond measures of public support to incorporate regional energy capacity, deployment targets and grid connection requests. Third-party datasets were integrated to add practical context, enabling users to explore where public backing aligns with infrastructure readiness—and where constraints or opportunities for intervention exist. Measures of technology potential were also introduced, including regional solar viability based on sunlight hours, allowing comparison between public attitudes and physical conditions for deployment.

The resulting tools support both high-level comparison and detailed regional analysis through interactive maps, national overviews and regional deep dives. Designed for stakeholders with varying levels of technical expertise, the dashboards prioritised clarity and usability while providing access to a wide range of underlying data. Together, they offered a practical framework for shaping campaign strategy, supporting local engagement and tracking progress towards EU 2030 targets.
