Data Visualisation Strategy

Building shared systems for how analysis is communicated and used

Data visualisation at Stonehaven operates as a core analytical capability. I lead this function, shaping how quantitative and qualitative insights are communicated across the organisation and to clients, with a focus on building shared systems that support consistent, high-quality communication of analysis over time.

From individual outputs to organisational capability

The work spans visual standards, tooling and training, grounded in the practical realities of how insights are produced and used. Rather than treating visualisation as a final step, the focus is on embedding it across analytical workflows so that outputs remain coherent, comparable and usable across projects and over time.

Data products and tooling

Data products are designed and developed across both business intelligence platforms and fully custom-coded tools, selected based on the client environment, requirements and use case. This includes dashboards and productised insight suites that support ongoing analysis, client engagement and day-to-day work.

Standards, systems and enablement

Alongside delivery, organisation-wide guidelines for data visualisation and storytelling have been established to support clarity and rigour across outputs. Custom charting libraries enables more bespoke visualisation while maintaining consistency, and shared templates support everyday use across tools such as PowerPoint and Excel.

Training and adoption

Training is delivered across both visual best practice and practical software use, enabling colleagues to translate complex analysis into accessible and impactful insights. This supports confident use of data products and helps teams articulate their value in client engagements.

Why this matters

Taken together, this work positions data visualisation as a connective layer between analysis, expertise and decision-making. By focusing on systems, standards and adoption, complex evidence can travel across teams, clients and contexts without losing meaning, rigour or impact.
